FAQ

Everything you need to know.

What is a Hackathon?

A hackathon is where you turn insane ideas into realities. Hundreds of hackers and makers from all over the world come together to build something exciting. There are judges and prizes, but the important part is learning in a fun and hands-on way.

Who can come?

College students and high schoolers! HackEmory applications are open to any enrolled college or high school student. Please show up with a valid student id, we'll be checking!

What if I don’t know how to code?

HackEmory is a great time and place to learn! We'll provide you with some getting started apps, and access to great workshops and mentors to help you build something impressive.

What if I don’t have a team or idea?

Don't worry! Most teams and ideas are formed at the event.

What should I bring?

At the very least, a valid student ID and a laptop- We'll provide the rest. A change of clothes and a pillow/sleeping bag are highly recommended. At the very most, well, we'll leave that up to you.

What can I build?

Anything you can build and present! Hardware, web, mobile, gaming, internet of things, the list goes on...

Help! I still have questions!

Shoot us an email! jake.cronin@emory.edu. He should get back to you within a day or so, sometimes just 5 minutes!!

What does it cost?

HackEmory is free for everyone that is accepted. Our job is to supply you with food, caffeine, swag, WiFi and a place to work. Your job is to change the world.

How do I sign up?

Scroll up to the apply button and tell us about yourself. You’ll hear back from us soon!
