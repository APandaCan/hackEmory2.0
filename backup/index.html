Let's learn by building.

Join us as we build apps, hardware, and community at Emory's 5fth annual hackathon! We’ll be hacking for over 24 hours straight, but we'll take some breaks to snack and goof off. Whether this is your 1st hackathon or your 314th, we'll be here to help every step of the way. Don't forget to bring a friend!

FAQ
What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is where you turn insane ideas into realities. Hundreds of hackers and makers from all over the world come together to build something exciting. At the end, there's a judging ceramony and prizes, but that isn't what its all about. Its about learning in a fun and hands-on way.

Who can come?

HackEmory apps are open to any enrolled college student (undergrad or grad), from all over the world. Please show up with a valid student id, we'll be checking!

What if I don’t know how to code?

HackEmory is a great time and place to learn! We'll provide you with some getting started apps, and access to great workshops and mentors to help you build something impressive.

What if I don’t have a team or idea?

Not to worry! Most teams and ideas are formed at the event.

What should I bring?

At the very least, a valid student ID and a laptop- We'll provide the rest. A change of clothes and a pillow/sleeping bag are highly recommended. At the very most, well, we'll leave that up to you.

What can I build?

Anything you can build then present! Hardware, web, mobile, gaming, internet of things, the list goes on...

What does it cost?

HackEmory is free for everyone that is accepted. We'll supply you with food, caffeine, swag, WiFi and a place to work. Travel reimbursements and bussing may also be available for qualifying participants.

What if I have some other question?

Shoot us an email! jake.cronin@emory.edu. He should get back to you within a day or so, sometimes just 5 minutes!

HackEmory

HackEmory is the premier club at Emory for software and robotics projects. Our club is made up of undergraduate students that have a passion for both software and hacking. We also throw an annual Hackathon at Emory.
